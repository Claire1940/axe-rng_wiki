<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Improve Chopping Efficiency">
    **Action:** Put early skill upgrades into anything that helps trees go down faster or makes each chopping cycle smoother. This includes upgrades to axe damage, swing speed, or critical chop chance.
    **Why it matters:** Chopping trees is the core active loop after rolling axes. Faster clearing directly improves every later goal by accelerating resource acquisition.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Support Resource Gain">
    **Action:** After basic chopping feels stable, focus on upgrades that help resource collection from repeated tree clears. This could involve increasing log drop rates, honey yield from bees, or overall resource multipliers.
    **Why it matters:** More resources provide the necessary currency for further upgrades, hatching more bees, and preparing for tougher zone progression.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Keep Rolling for Stronger Axes">
    **Action:** Use skill progress to support better axe rolls instead of relying only on your starting axe. Invest in upgrades that increase your luck or reduce the cost of rolling.
    **Why it matters:** The official loop starts with rolling powerful axes. Stronger axes make tree and zone progression significantly easier and faster.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Add Bees into the Routine">
    **Action:** Once chopping is consistent, hatch bees and keep collecting honey while you continue rolling and clearing trees. Upgrade bee-related skills to boost honey production.
    **Why it matters:** Bees and honey are a vital part of the progression loop, providing a parallel stream of resources that contribute to long-term growth.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Use Rebirth When Progress Slows">
    **Action:** Rebirth after your current axe and skill setup stops pushing zones efficiently. Prioritize upgrades that enhance rebirth benefits, such as increased luck per rebirth.
    **Why it matters:** Rebirth increases Luck, which is critical for future rolling attempts and long-term progression. It ensures you don't hit a permanent wall.
  </Step>
</Steps>
