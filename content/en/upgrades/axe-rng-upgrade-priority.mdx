<Section icon={Target} title="Skill Upgrade Order for Early Game Efficiency">

Optimizing your skill upgrades from the start can significantly accelerate your progress. This section breaks down the recommended order for investing your hard-earned resources.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Improve Chopping Efficiency">
    Your very first priority should be any skill that makes trees fall faster or reduces the effort required to clear them. This includes upgrades to axe swing speed, damage, or critical hit chance. Faster chopping directly translates to more resources per minute.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Support Resource Gain">
    Once your basic chopping feels stable and efficient, shift your focus to skills that enhance resource collection. This could involve upgrades that increase the amount of wood dropped, boost honey production, or provide bonus materials. More resources mean more upgrades, faster bee hatching, and better preparation for new zones.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Keep Rolling for Stronger Axes">
    While not a direct skill upgrade, maintaining your ability to roll for better axes is crucial. Invest in Luck-related skills or ensure you have enough resources to utilize Luck boosts. Stronger axes are fundamental to progressing through tougher trees and zones.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Add Bees into the Routine">
    Once chopping is consistent, begin investing in bee-related skills and hatching bees. Honey is a vital secondary resource in Axe RNG, and integrating bee collection early ensures a steady parallel income stream.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Use Rebirth When Progress Slows">
    Rebirth is not an upgrade in the traditional sense, but a progression mechanic that resets your progress in exchange for a significant Luck boost. Time your rebirths strategically when your current axe and skill setup can no longer efficiently push into new zones. This ensures future rolls yield better axes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Mistake">
Many players neglect chopping efficiency in favor of other upgrades too early. Remember, **faster chopping fuels everything else**. Without it, other upgrades become less impactful.
</Callout>

</Section>
